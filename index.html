Beatwave- A Motion Tracking DJ App project

UX Design and Research

Recent advances in consumer grade motion capture technologies have created opportunities for more expressive and accessible DJing applications. Over the course of the semester, my team engaged with DJs to identify and their needs and tested prototypes to determine the most appropriate motion tracking technology.

At the end of our project, DJs found that Beatwave exceeded their existing hardware for its performative qualities and enjoyability.

Amazon UX Design Internship 2015

UX and Visual design

In the summer of 2015, I designed a feature for Amazon Marketing Services. First, I conducted and compiled competitive research and user research. Using this research and feedback from other designers and developers, I iteratively developed an interactive protype. I updated the design based on user testing and submitted a finalized interactive prototype.

The feature hasn't been released yet. Users showed enthusiasm for it during testing, and I successfully promoted the most user-centered of the solutions proposed to leadership.

Sharacter- An Interactive Drawing iOS Application

UX and Visual Design

We wanted an accessible, easy to use, collaborative drawing game that would facilitate creativity and help people connect with distant friends and family. I conducted competitive research and developed the design for the current version.

Sharacter is a work in progress, but so far our test users have expressed the desire to invite their friends to play.

Amaze Game Jam

UX and Visual design

Amaze is a game I designed for the 2014 UC Berkeley UPE Game Jam, a 24 hour event, with my teammate, Anton Holmberg. Anton already had an idea for a game, so we identified the constraints (time, skills, and the most appropriate platform and aesthetic for that type of game). I designed the interface and graphics and Anton coded the game using Objective C.

Amaze won third place in the game jam.
