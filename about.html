Background

My academic background in neurobiology, art, and 3D animation naturally led me to pursue user experience design. I have worked in multiple research and art settings, including the Animation Research Labs at the University of Washington. I am now a student in the Master of Information Management and Systems program at UC Berkeley, where I integrate psychology, art, and research to design interactive experiences to enable creativity and scientific thinking.

Recently, I have focused on education both as an instructor and a researcher. I am currently a graduate student researcher in the School of Information, where I study tools for youth data literacy and youth driven tech projects. I also tutor for Interface Aesthetics, a graphic design class in the School of Information, and work with Bay Area youth on self-driven tech projects.
